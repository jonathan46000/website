Volumetric Display Project

This will eventually be a persistence of vision volumetric display using LED Controllers spiraled up a shaft. Right now we just have the LED controller which consists of shift registers and RGB LEDs. My buddy Paul and myself have designed this project. The first image on this page is toner transferred to the top of a copper clad PCB using a laminator and special paper.

After the toner is on the PCB it's washed in Ferric Acid at 120 degrees Farenheight for about 5 minutes. This washes away all of the copper that isn't protected by toner. This is a surface mount project so in the final phase all of the controllers will have there components placed on a solder pasted board and placed in an oven to reflow.

Currently we have working prototypes of the controllers but have not made a full length board or all of the fins needed to complete the spiral. Eventually all of these fins will be stack up in a spiral and spun fast enough to make 3D images. You can see the light test in the image of the working board below.

We intend to use a PIC32 or ARM processor to drive the display. Stretch goals may be making it able to take images over USB from a computer that have been made in blender or autocad.
