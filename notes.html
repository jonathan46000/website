7/4/2017
Many things have happened since the last time I blogged on here. I have visited iceland, attended RVASec 2017 as a developer for the RVASec badges, started a new software project and have become the president of the Linux Users Group at the college I attend. First of all, Iceland is a beautiful country and if you ever have the chance you should go there. Similarly RVASec 2017 was a blast and the HackRVA badges were a hit once again. Two of my apps made it into the badge this year. You can find the source code for the entire badge at https://github.com/HackRVA/Harmony-Badge-2017-public and the code to my drawing app for the badge at https://github.com/HackRVA/Harmony-Badge-2017-public/blob/master/firmware/src/badge_apps/udraw.c.

Running a Linux Users Group has tought me a lot. I have never claimed to be an expert at Linux(most linux users would never dare claim to be an expert). I am surprised however at how much you can learn by helping others. This has been a very fulfilling experience for me.

01/10/2016
This entry will contain some recent reviews of some software I have tried out and some notes on updates that have been made since the last entry.

UPDATES: Since the last time I posted on here the domain www.jonathan46000.com has been purchased and the site updated accordingly.

GENTOO REVIEW: I recently installed Gentoo Linux on a vm to see what it was like to do a Gentoo install. I found it somewhat similar to installing Arch. I do have one major issue with Gentoo and that is that portage/emerge is absolutely awful and contains none of the well thought out dependency resolution and installation options that Pacman offers. Not considering its painful package managing system though I would say that Gentoo is as well put together as any other stripped down targeted distro that I have used and to some degree better. You do end up with a more trimmed down tailored distro than you do with Arch but not by much. I don't think I could use this as my daily driver without more work on the package management side.

PLASMA5 REVIEW: Most people who know me well know that KDE has been my desktop environment of choice for many years now. I don't even want to go into all of the things I hate about Gnome right now and XFCE just doesn't provide me the full featured rich environment that I desire. Needless to say I don't do my computing on sluggish hardware. The transition to Plasma 5 is much less painful than the upgrade to KDE 4 was. Everything worked great and the only hurdle was they are not co-installable so there is that hold your breath moment where you have to uninstall you DE and hope that the one you are about to install will function when you reboot. Everything not only worked as expected it worked better. Plasma 5 is remarkably stable and much faster than KDE4. It has a very modern and functional feel and the convenient functionality that has been added makes it a pleasurable experience to use.
