Torque Loader Project

When we needed to find a way to repeatably apply 1000ft-lbs of torque to a torque transducer to calibrate wrenches the costs were much higher than we had originally anticipated to buy an off the shelf solution. I desgined this torque loader made of steel plates, threaded rod and and some other random parts we purchased from Lowes.

There are two threaded rods above the table and one below to hold it in place. There is a thick threaded rod that has been threaded through bolts in the central loading piece that were welded on by my wife. The smooth rod at the top is to keep it tracked to force it to move when you turn the handle connected to the threaded rod. As the handel rotates the piece in the middle moves from one side to the other as the threaded rod spins. The threaded rod is connected to the endplates through bearings and double nutted ends.
